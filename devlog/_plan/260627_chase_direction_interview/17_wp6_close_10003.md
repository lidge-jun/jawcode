## The bug (deterministic, audit-provable)

Cursor's wire protocol carries shell timeouts in **milliseconds**; the bash tool's `timeout`
expects **seconds**. jwc `packages/coding-agent/src/cursor.ts` passes `args.timeout` (ms) straight
through as seconds at **2 call sites** (L224 `shell`, L246 `shellStream`): a 30 s (30000 ms) wait
becomes 30000 s, clamped to the 3600 s ceiling → an accidental ~1-hour timeout. gjc fixed this with
a `shellTimeoutSeconds` helper (`Math.max(1, Math.ceil(timeout/1000))`); jwc lacks it (gap confirmed).

## Changes — `packages/coding-agent/src/cursor.ts` (only)

1. Add module-level `shellTimeoutSeconds(timeout: number | undefined): number | undefined` —
   faithful port of the reviewed gjc helper (with the explanatory doc comment), placed near the
   other module-level helpers before the class.
2. Replace both `const timeoutSeconds = args.timeout && args.timeout > 0 ? args.timeout : undefined;`
   (L224, L246) with `const timeoutSeconds = shellTimeoutSeconds(args.timeout);`.
